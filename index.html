About

I am Niranjan Simha D H, a 2nd-year BCA student at SDM College, Ujire, and an artist deeply inspired by Indian temple culture and the timeless wisdom of Shilpashastra — the traditional science of art, sculpture, and architecture. My drawings reflect a personal journey of learning and devotion, rooted in the sacred forms, patterns, and philosophies found in classical Indian.

Through my work, I explore the symbolic language of temples, sacred geometry, and traditional iconography. I aim to honor and reinterpret these traditions, keeping them alive in a modern context while staying true to their spiritual essence.

Alongside my academic journey, I have a growing interest in Data Science and modern technologies. I am fascinated by how data and analytics can uncover patterns, tell stories, and bring structure to the complexities of the modern world — much like the intricate order and symbolism found in temple architecture.

As a lifelong learner, I am constantly seeking knowledge, refining my artistic practice, and deepening my understanding of both the cultural and technological foundations that shape our times. For me, art and technology are not separate, but complementary paths of exploration: one connects me to ancient wisdom and spiritual depth, while the other opens doors to modern innovation and insight.

Art, for me, is not just a form of expression — it is a way of connecting to something greater, a bridge between the visible and the invisible, the past and the present. By combining my passion for traditional art with my curiosity for data science, I hope to create a unique narrative that reflects both my roots and my vision for the future.
